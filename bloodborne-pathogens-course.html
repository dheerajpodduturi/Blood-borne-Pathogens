Reference Material

Key Pathogens at a Glance

Expand each section to learn about the major bloodborne pathogens, their characteristics, and key facts.

Hepatitis B Virus (HBV)
Transmission RoutesBlood, semen, vaginal fluids, and other body fluids through percutaneous or mucosal exposure
Survivability on SurfacesCan survive outside the body for at least 7 days and still be capable of causing infection
Vaccine AvailabilityYes — highly effective 3-dose vaccine series available. OSHA requires employers to offer free HBV vaccination.
Incubation Period45 to 160 days (average 120 days)
Chronic Infection Rate2-6% of adults develop chronic infection
Hepatitis C Virus (HCV)
Transmission RoutesPrimarily through percutaneous exposure to infected blood (needlestick injuries)
Survivability on SurfacesCan survive on environmental surfaces at room temperature for up to 3 weeks
Vaccine AvailabilityNo vaccine available. Antiviral treatments can cure over 95% of cases.
Incubation Period14 to 180 days (average 45 days)
Chronic Infection Rate75-85% of infected persons develop chronic infection
Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV)
Transmission RoutesBlood, semen, vaginal fluids, breast milk through percutaneous or mucosal exposure
Survivability on SurfacesFragile virus — does not survive well outside the body. Inactivated quickly when exposed to air.
Vaccine AvailabilityNo vaccine available. Post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) can reduce risk if started within 72 hours.
Incubation Period2 to 4 weeks for acute symptoms; may remain asymptomatic for years
Occupational RiskApproximately 0.3% risk from a single needlestick with HIV-infected blood
Other Bloodborne Pathogens

While HBV, HCV, and HIV are the primary concerns, other pathogens can also be transmitted through blood:

Hepatitis D (HDV)Occurs only in those already infected with HBV. Vaccination against HBV prevents HDV.
SyphilisCaused by Treponema pallidum bacteria, transmissible via blood contact.
MalariaCaused by Plasmodium parasites, can be transmitted through blood transfusion or needlestick.
BrucellosisBacterial infection that can be transmitted through contact with infected blood.
